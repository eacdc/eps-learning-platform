2. Information we collect

We collect information in the following ways:

2.1 Account and profile information

  • Information you provide when you register or sign in (for example, name, email address, username, and similar account details).
  • Profile photograph: If you choose to upload or capture a profile picture, we process that image to display your profile and to store it if you upload it to our servers (see Section 3).

2.2 Sign-in with Google

If you choose Google sign-in, Google provides authentication information to the App according to your choices and Google’s settings. We may receive identifiers such as your Google account email and name as permitted by Google and your consent.

Google’s use of information is described in Google’s policies: Google Privacy Policy.

2.3 Learning activity and app content

  • Information related to your use of learning features (for example, books, chapters, chat messages you send within the App, quiz or score-related data, and similar activity needed to provide the service).
  • Notifications or in-app messages related to your account or learning progress, where applicable.

2.4 Camera (QR scanning)

The App may request access to the device camera to scan QR codes (for example, to add or open learning materials). Camera input is used for scanning in the App as part of providing that feature.

2.5 Microphone and audio

The App may request access to the microphone to record audio when you use voice features. Audio may be sent to our backend to generate transcripts or responses needed to provide the learning/chat features.

2.6 Device data and diagnostics

  • Basic technical information needed to operate the App (for example, device type, operating system version, app version, and security-related logs where necessary).
  • Network requests are made over the internet; standard connection metadata may be processed by our hosting providers.

2.7 Local storage on your device

The App may store session tokens, preferences, or similar data on your device using local storage mechanisms (for example, secure storage or shared preferences) to keep you signed in and remember settings.
